pH

pH what? You don’t need a PhD to understand pH!

pH

If you’re doing any kind of growing, you’ve likely heard of pH. But what is pH and why is it important?KEY POINTS

What is pH? pH is a scale (0-14) which indicates how acidic or basic a solution is (water in our case). pH stands for power of Hydrogen and is a measurement of hydrogen ions. Less then 7 is acidic, greater than 7 is basic (no basic bitches here), and 7 is neutral.

The scale is logarithmic, if the pH changes by 1, it 10x more intense. A pH of 6 is 10x more acidic than 7. A pH of 5 is 100x more acidic than 7, 4 is 1000x more and so on. I'll explain why this important later.

Why do we care about pH? As pH changes, so does the chemical composition of the soil, which impacts the availability of nutrients. Worst case scenario, your pH is so far off that nutrients are not available to your plant, commonly referred as nutrient lockout in the cannabis industry.

Without nutrients, your plant will grow well. We want a pH that maximizes the number of nutrients available.

The magic number. Like most plants, cannabis thrives in slightly acidic soil. A pH range of 6-7 is optimal. Some strains will prefer closer to 6 while other closer to 7.

Measuring pH. The following methods are the most common.

  • digital meter
  • pH probe
  • litmus strips
  • mixing kit
Digital meters are dipped in water and provide instant and accurate readings. Meters require recalibration from time to time, which you can do yourself. Follow the instructions provided with your meter.

A pH probe is pushed into the soil and reads pH directly. As with digital meters, their accuracy is highly dependant on their quality.

A litmus strip (think highschool science class) is a cheap way to give you an idea of the pH. They are dipped in water and change colour but may not be very accurate.

Mixing kits give more accurate results, but rely on the user to identify the pH by colour. If you’re colour blind (like me) this can cause some problems.

We recommend investing in a high quality digital meter and checking it against a mixing kit and/or litmus strips. Check the pH of your water after adding nutrients.

Correcting pH. The easiest way to adjust pH is to buy solutions to raise and lower. Keep in mind these solutions are VERY STRONG and a little goes a long way. Add sparingly, measure and adjust.

Key Points

  • pH = power of Hydrogen (concentration of hydrogen ions).
  • 0-14 scale, less than 7 is acidic, greater than 7 is basic, 7 is neutral.
  • Scale is logarithmic - a change in pH of 1 is equal to 10 times.
  • Marijuana prefers ~6.5-6.8
  • Invest is a quality meter and stay on top of it, it’s a small amount of work to avoid big headaches.
  • Adjust pH gradually so you do not shock your plants.
